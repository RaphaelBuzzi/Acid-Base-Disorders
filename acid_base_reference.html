Acid–Base Analysis

Step-by-Step Interpretation

Step 1 — Check pH
↓
pH < 7.35 Acidemia
↓
↓ HCO₃⁻ Metabolic acidosis
↑ PaCO₂ Resp. acidosis
pH > 7.45 Alkalemia
↓
↑ HCO₃⁻ Metabolic alkalosis
↓ PaCO₂ Resp. alkalosis
↓
Step 3 — Anion Gap (if met. acidosis)
↓
AG = Na⁺ − (Cl⁻ + HCO₃⁻)
↓
AG > 12 High anion gap
→ GOLDMARK
AG ≤ 12 Normal anion gap
→ HARDUP
↓
Step 4 — Check Compensation
↓
Winter's formula PaCO₂ = 0.2 × HCO₃ + 1.1 ± 0.3 kPa Mismatch → suggests mixed acid–base disorder

  • OOxoprolineChronic paracetamol use
  • LL-LactateSepsis, shock, ischaemia
  • DD-LactateShort bowel syndrome
  • MMethanolToxic alcohol ingestion
  • AAspirinSalicylate toxicity
